AI for Productivity:
Work Smarter, Not Harder

You don't need to be a data scientist to use AI. Here's exactly how I use it every day, and how you can start today.

Lissett Gamboa Luna
Data Scientist & AI Enthusiast
📅 June 2025
⏱ 7 min read

Let me be honest with you. A couple of years ago, I was the person working 10-hour days, drowning in repetitive tasks, and wondering how some people seemed to get twice as much done in half the time. Then AI happened, not dramatically, not overnight, but slowly and practically.

I'm a data scientist. I work with models, pipelines, and code daily. But the AI tools that changed my life weren't the cutting-edge research papers. They were the simple, accessible ones that anyone can start using today. This post is about those.

💡 The Big Idea

AI isn't here to replace you. It's here to take the boring parts off your plate so you can focus on what actually matters, the creative, strategic, human stuff.

The Shift That Changed Everything

I used to spend a lot of time on things that felt productive but weren't really: writing the same type of email over and over, summarizing long reports, searching for how to write a formula I'd written a hundred times before. Small things, but they added up to hours every week.

The moment I started thinking of AI as a thinking partner, not a magic button, everything changed. The key word is "partner." You bring the context, the judgment, and the goal. AI brings the speed, the options, and the drafts. Together, you move faster than either of you could alone.

Real Examples From My Week

Let me make this concrete. Here's how AI showed up in my work this week alone:

Monday: Had a 45-page supplier report to read before a meeting. I pasted the key sections into Claude and asked: "What are the three most important points and what questions should I ask?" Meeting prep done in 10 minutes instead of an hour.

Tuesday: Needed to send a follow-up email to a client but wasn't sure how to phrase some difficult feedback diplomatically. I wrote what I wanted to say roughly, asked Claude to "make this constructive and professional without losing the key message." First draft done, light editing, sent.

Thursday: My automated n8n workflow for inventory reports ran on its own, compiled the data, and sent a summary to the team. I did zero work. It just happened.

⏱ Time saved estimate

Across these three examples alone: roughly 4–5 hours I got back. Every week. That's more than 200 hours per year, time I now spend on deeper work, learning, or just not burning out.

How to Start Today (3 Steps)

  1. Pick one task you repeat every week, it could be writing a status update, summarizing a document, or searching for something. That's your first target.
  2. Try it with a free AI assistant, Claude or ChatGPT both have free tiers. Don't overthink the prompt. Just talk to it like you'd talk to a smart colleague. Describe what you need and what the context is.
  3. Notice what happened, Was it faster? Did it open up time? Did the output need editing? That feedback loop is how you get better at using AI effectively.

That's it. Start with one task. The toolkit grows naturally as you go.

What AI Can't Replace

I want to be clear about something: AI is a tool, not a replacement for judgment, relationships, or creativity. It gives you options, you choose. It drafts, you decide. It automates the routine, you handle the nuance.

The people who will thrive in the next decade aren't those who avoid AI or those who blindly trust it. They're the ones who know when to use it, how to guide it, and when to set it aside. That skill, knowing where the human judgment is irreplaceable, is more valuable than any tool.

Ejemplos Reales de Mi Semana

Quiero ser concreta. Así apareció la IA en mi trabajo solo esta semana:

Lunes: Tenía un informe de 45 páginas de proveedor que leer antes de una reunión. Pegué las secciones clave en Claude y pregunté: "¿Cuáles son los tres puntos más importantes y qué preguntas debería hacer?" La preparación terminó en 10 minutos en lugar de una hora.

Martes: Necesitaba enviar un email de seguimiento a un cliente pero no sabía cómo formular cierto feedback difícil de forma diplomática. Escribí lo que quería decir en bruto y le pedí a Claude que lo hiciera "constructivo y profesional sin perder el mensaje clave." Primer borrador listo, edición ligera, enviado.

Jueves: Mi flujo automatizado de n8n para informes de inventario corrió solo, recopiló los datos y envió un resumen al equipo. Yo no hice nada. Simplemente ocurrió.

⏱ Estimación de tiempo ahorrado

Solo con estos tres ejemplos: aproximadamente 4–5 horas recuperadas. Cada semana. Son más de 200 horas al año, tiempo que ahora dedico a trabajo más profundo, a aprender, o simplemente a no quemarme.

Cómo Empezar Hoy (3 Pasos)

  1. Elige una tarea que repites cada semana, puede ser escribir un update de estado, resumir un documento, o buscar algo. Ese es tu primer objetivo.
  2. Pruébalo con un asistente de IA gratuito, Claude y ChatGPT tienen versiones gratuitas. No te compliques con el prompt. Háblale como lo harías con un colega inteligente. Describe lo que necesitas y cuál es el contexto.
  3. Observa qué pasó, ¿Fue más rápido? ¿Liberó tiempo? ¿El resultado necesitaba edición? Ese bucle de retroalimentación es cómo mejoras usando la IA de forma efectiva.

Eso es todo. Empieza con una tarea. El kit crece de forma natural a medida que avanzas.

Lo que la IA No Puede Reemplazar

Quiero ser clara en algo: la IA es una herramienta, no un reemplazo para el juicio, las relaciones o la creatividad. Te da opciones, tú eliges. Hace borradores, tú decides. Automatiza lo rutinario, tú gestionas los matices.

Las personas que prosperarán en la próxima década no son las que evitan la IA ni las que confían en ella ciegamente. Son las que saben cuándo usarla, cómo guiarla y cuándo dejarla de lado. Esa habilidad, saber dónde el juicio humano es irremplazable, vale más que cualquier herramienta.
